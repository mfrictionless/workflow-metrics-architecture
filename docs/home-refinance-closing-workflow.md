# Home Refinance Closing Workflow

The following workflow covers a typical U.S. residential mortgage refinance from application through post-closing. Exact requirements and timing vary by lender, loan program, and state.

| Step | Activity | Primary responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Apply | Submit the loan application and authorize credit, income, asset, and property verification. | Borrower; Loan officer assists |
| 2. Disclose and acknowledge | Issue the Loan Estimate and required disclosures; review and acknowledge receipt. | Lender issues; Borrower reviews |
| 3. Process the loan | Collect and validate income, assets, insurance, mortgage, and property information. | Loan processor; Borrower supplies requested documents |
| 4. Appraise the property | Determine the property's current market value when an appraisal or other valuation is required. | Appraiser or valuation provider; Lender orders |
| 5. Complete title work | Search title, identify liens or ownership issues, obtain payoff information, and prepare the title commitment. | Title/settlement company |
| 6. Underwrite | Evaluate credit, capacity, collateral, and compliance; issue conditions or approval. | Lender's underwriter |
| 7. Clear conditions | Resolve underwriting and title requirements and confirm the loan is clear to close. | Loan processor coordinates; Borrower, lender, and title company provide items |
| 8. Prepare closing | Set the signing date, finalize figures, and deliver the Closing Disclosure for review within the required timeframe. | Lender and title/settlement company; Borrower reviews |
| 9. Sign | Verify identity, sign the loan and security documents, and provide any required funds. | Borrower; Notary or settlement agent conducts signing |
| 10. Complete rescission period | For an eligible refinance of a primary residence, allow the borrower the applicable right-to-cancel period before disbursement. | Lender/settlement company monitors; Borrower may cancel |
| 11. Fund and disburse | Fund the new loan, pay off the existing mortgage and other approved obligations, and send any proceeds to the borrower. | Lender funds; Title/settlement company disburses |
| 12. Record and close | Record the new mortgage or deed of trust, confirm payoff, issue the lender's title policy, and retain the final documents. | Title/settlement company; County recorder records |
